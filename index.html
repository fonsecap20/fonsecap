Legend(s) of Zelda

Made as a class project for EECS 494: Intro to Game Development. Features the first dungeon of the original NES class "The Legend of Zelda" and a custom level where you might feel like you are seeing double. Thank you Melissa Fang for being a great partner!

Contributions

Like the shiny weapons? I handled all of their functionaility and animations. The Legend of Zelda is full of rich sound effects and I implemented them in this rendition of the classic game. Condition based events like opening locked doors, puzzle doors, or enemy-locked doors were part of my duties. The nostalgic Old Man Room, the perspective-shifting 2D Bow Room, and everything inside of them (besides enemies) also fell into my hands.

Toolkit

My partner and I created this version of "The Legend of Zelda" through the Unity game engine and C# scripts. Project management was handled through the Jira Software. A WebGL build was then hosted to itch.io which you may find below.

Left Behind

Made as a class project for EECS 494: Intro to Game Development. Features a mix of known stamina mechanics and temporary invincibility mechanics with a flying auto-scroller.

Contributions

This project was a solo project. Other than the art and audio, I am the sole owner of all the details seen in-game. Some noteworthy methods utilized in the game's creation include level segmentation (for performance maintenance), ample resource placement, semi-random but accuracte obstacle spawning, and a singelton GameManager procedure. Special thanks to a close friend Dillon Hembree for the bird art.
